<section class="cbl-s05-join-section" aria-labelledby="join-openings-heading">
  <h2 id="join-openings-heading">Openings</h2>
  <article class="cbl-s05-role-card">
    <span class="cbl-card-kicker">Research Assistant / Research Associate</span>
    <h3>Science of Learning of Emerging Skills</h3>
    <p>The Centre for Research and Development in Learning at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore (CRADLE@NTU) seeks applicants with a Bachelor's or Master's degree in Psychology, Cognitive Neuroscience, or a related field from a university of good standing for a Research Assistant / Research Associate position.</p>
    <p>The successful candidate will be an integral member of an interdisciplinary research team studying the Science of Learning of emerging skills, including literacy and numeracy. The role requires task orientation, excellent organizational skills, attention to detail and accuracy, flexibility in fluid situations, good interpersonal skills, and the ability to work independently. Experience with cognitive or neuropsychological testing and/or neuroimaging techniques, such as functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS), functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI), diffusion MRI (DTI, HARDI, DSI), is required.</p>
    <h4>Key Responsibilities</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Participant recruitment.</li>
      <li>Data collection using psychological and neuroimaging techniques, such as cognitive testing, fNIRS, and MRI.</li>
      <li>Perform data analyses and manage data analysis pipelines under the PI's supervision.</li>
      <li>Perform research-related administrative work, such as purchase of material or equipment for research.</li>
      <li>Liaise and work closely with stakeholders from external institutions, including schools, and project collaborators.</li>
      <li>Conduct literature reviews and prepare policy reports for stakeholders.</li>
      <li>Prepare presentations and manuscripts for dissemination of findings.</li>
      <li>Support systems administration, including maintenance of network servers and data security.</li>
      <li>Carry out any other duties as required by the Principal Investigator.</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Job Requirements</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Excellent communication skills and a good command of English to perform literature reviews, write progress reports, and present research findings.</li>
      <li>Self-motivated and able to work independently to collect data, analyse data, and communicate findings.</li>
      <li>Proficiency in statistical analysis, such as SPSS or R, programming languages, such as Python or Matlab, and experience with neuroimaging software.</li>
      <li>Willingness to travel to testing sites for data collection.</li>
      <li>Excellent organizational skills and ability to work within tight deadlines.</li>
      <li>Self-directed learner who effectively picks up relevant skills as needed.</li>
      <li>Prior experience with cognitive neuroscience research, neuroimaging techniques, such as fNIRS or MRI, and academic publication processes.</li>
    </ul>
    <p>Interested applicants should email a CV, a brief cover letter, and at least two letters of recommendation to <a href="mailto:annabelchen@ntu.edu.sg">annabelchen@ntu.edu.sg</a>. We regret that only shortlisted candidates will be notified.</p>
  </article>
</section>

  <h2 id="join-ntu-heading">Interdisciplinary Brain Science at NTU</h2>
  <p>NTU is a strong home for ambitious researchers who want to build rigorous science across disciplinary boundaries. Graduate studies can draw on a research-intensive environment spanning psychology, neuroscience, medicine, education, engineering, social sciences, and translational research.</p>
  <p>Through the Cognitive Neuroimaging Centre (CoNiC), graduate researchers can work with advanced neuroimaging and neuromodulation facilities, including MRI, EEG, MEG, fNIRS, TMS, and tDCS. This ecosystem is well suited for people who want deep methodological training while developing research with clinical, educational, or community impact.</p>
